🛂
Section 01
Passport
パスポートについて
01
Validity
Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your return date. Please check the expiry date well in advance and renew if necessary.
Blank Pages
Ensure you have at least 2 blank pages available for stamps.
Visa
Many nationalities can enter Japan visa-free for up to 90 days for tourism. Please verify the entry requirements for your specific nationality before travel.
⚠ Important
Please carry your passport at all times while in Japan. A copy stored separately (e.g., in a hotel safe or digital photo) is strongly recommended.
🏥
Section 02
Travel Insurance
海外旅行保険について
02
Required
Travel insurance is mandatory for all tour participants. Each participant must arrange their own individual policy.
Coverage
Your policy must include coverage for trail running / mountain sports activities. Standard travel insurance may not cover sports-related injuries — please confirm with your insurer.
Recommended
Coverage for: Medical expensesEmergency evacuationTrip cancellationLost baggageSports injury
Submission
Your policy number and insurer contact details will be required prior to the tour departure date. Please have this information ready.
⚠ Note
Participants without valid travel insurance will not be permitted to join the tour. Please arrange your policy as early as possible.

Section 03
Currency & Exchange
日本円への両替
03
Currency
Japan's currency is the Japanese Yen (JPY / ¥).
Exchange Tips
Exchange rates at airports in Japan tend to be competitive. Alternatively, 7-Eleven and Japan Post ATMs widely accept international cards and offer favorable rates.
How Much?
We recommend carrying approximately ¥10,000–¥20,000 in cash for daily use (meals, local transport, vending machines, etc.).
Card Usage
富士河口湖周辺
Around Fujikawaguchiko (Mt. Fuji area), the mix of card and cash acceptance is as follows:
💳 Card Accepted
Larger hotels & restaurants
Major convenience stores (7-Eleven, FamilyMart, Lawson), chain restaurants, tourist facilities
💴 Cash Preferred
Local & smaller shops
Small local eateries, roadside stalls, vending machines, local markets, some onsen facilities
ℹ Recommendation
While card payments are becoming more common, cash is still essential in the Fujikawaguchiko area. Always carry some Japanese Yen to avoid inconvenience, especially at smaller local establishments.

Section 04
Wi-Fi & Mobile Connectivity
WiFi・モバイル通信について
04
Accommodation
PICA Fujiyoshida (your accommodation) provides free Wi-Fi throughout the facility.
Race Course
The race takes place in mountain terrain. There is no Wi-Fi available on the course. Mobile signal may also be weak or completely absent in forested and mountainous sections.
Recommended
We strongly recommend arranging mobile data connectivity before departure. Options include:

eSIM — Purchase a Japan eSIM online in advance (most convenient)
Pocket Wi-Fi — Rent at Japanese airports
SIM Card — Japan data SIM available at airports and electronics stores
⚠ Safety Note
Having mobile data access is essential for safety during trail running events. Please ensure you have a working solution before race day. An eSIM purchased in advance is the most hassle-free option.
📡 IBUKI GPS
Mt.FUJI100 officially offers an optional IBUKI GPS rental service.
IBUKI is a real-time GPS tracking system — by carrying the device during the race, your location is transmitted every 3 minutes, allowing supporters and family anywhere in the world to follow your progress on a live map.
Availability
Optional service for the first 1,000 applicants. Available for all race categories: FUJI100mi, KAI70k, and ASUMI40k. Separate advance application required.
Rental Fee
FUJI100mi ¥4,400 KAI70k ¥3,300 ASUMI40k ¥1,800
All prices tax included. Fee payable at time of application.
How It Works
Pick up the device (already powered on) at the mandatory equipment check / bib exchange booth on race day. Attach it to your pack's shoulder strap using the dedicated pouch. Return at the goal venue after finishing.
Battery Note
Battery lasts approximately 27–30 hours from power-on. Since the device is on during the pre-race wait, FUJI100mi participants should top up with a portable battery until just before the start, and carry one during the race for occasional charging.
Apply
Register via the official IBUKI page linked on the Mt.FUJI100 website:
mtfuji100.com → IBUKI GPS Optional Rental banner
ℹ Note — Tracking Only
At Mt.FUJI100, IBUKI is used for tracking purposes only and is not used for official time measurement. It is not mandatory, but is strongly recommended for the benefit of your support crew and loved ones following from home.

Section 06
Japan Arrival Procedures
日本への入国手続きについて
06
Visit Japan Web
Japan now uses the Visit Japan Web system for digital arrival procedures. Pre-registering online speeds up immigration and customs significantly.

Register at: vjw-lp.digital.go.jp
What to Register
Immigration (Entry)Customs DeclarationTax Refund (optional)
On the Plane
Some airlines still distribute paper Customs Declaration forms on board. Fill these in if provided — one form per family/travelling group.
At Immigration
You will need to:
• Present your passport
• Have fingerprints scanned (both index fingers)
• Have your photo taken
• State your purpose of visit: "Tourism / Sports event"
Customs
Declare any items over duty-free limits. Food items brought into Japan should also be declared if required. Most standard running gear and personal effects require no declaration.
ℹ Tip
Registering on Visit Japan Web before departure allows you to show a QR code at the immigration and customs counters — significantly reducing waiting times at the airport.
